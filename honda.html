About Honda


Honda Motor Co., Ltd., founded in 1948, is a Japanese public multinational conglomerate best known as a manufacturer of automobiles, motorcycles, and power equipment. With its headquarters in Tokyo, Japan, Honda has been the world's largest motorcycle manufacturer since 1959. It also became the second-largest Japanese automobile manufacturer in 2001 and was the eighth-largest automobile manufacturer in the world in 2015.

Beyond vehicles, Honda's expansive portfolio includes producing garden equipment, marine engines, personal watercraft, power generators, and other products. Over time, Honda has ventured into robotics, aerospace, and the AI industry.

Soichiro Honda and Takeo Fujisawa founded the company, with the former playing the role of a visionary who was passionate about creating efficient and affordable mobility solutions. The brand's first product was a bicycle engine, which eventually led to the creation of their first motorcycle. Today, Honda vehicles and products are recognized for their efficiency, reliability, and innovative engineering.

In terms of automobiles, the Honda Civic, Accord, and CR-V are among the most popular and best-selling models in many parts of the world. Honda's emphasis on research and development has also pushed them towards creating hybrid electric vehicles, hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, and the exploration of other alternative fuel options.

Throughout its history, Honda has maintained a strong reputation for producing reliable and fuel-efficient vehicles and engines. Its products resonate with consumers looking for durability, making Honda a household name in many countries.
